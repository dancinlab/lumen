# H_B2 — high-cross-section ultrathin resist

> **🜂 ABSTRACT.** Brainstorm shortlist (`state/resist-laser-brainstorm.md`, C2). Unverified.

## Which wall
Dose (demand side) — make each photon count, so a starved source suffices.

## Principle
Metal-oxide (Hf/Zr/Sn-oxo) clusters absorb EUV 4–6× more than organic CAR; a metamaterial
impedance-matched "EUV-black" film can approach near-total absorption in a ≤15 nm layer. More
absorbed energy per incident photon → lower dose-to-clear → lower required source average power.

## Falsifiable prediction
A ≤12 nm high-cross-section resist hits target CD at ≤ 50% the incident photon budget of a 30 nm
spin-on CAR (i.e. ≥2× effective dose), at equal LER. If stochastics/LER degrade faster than dose
falls, falsified.

## How it would be tested
Absorption (cross-section × thickness, Beer–Lambert) × quantum-yield model vs dose-to-clear;
metamaterial absorber is the falsifiable stretch (≥95% in <15 nm). Not run here.

## Honest status
Unverified (🜂); MOx resists are real and adopted, the ultrathin/metamaterial limit is the stretch.
The RLS (resolution–LER–sensitivity) trade is the open risk.
