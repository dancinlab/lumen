# H_B4 — re-invertible high-rep recombination EUV laser

> **🜂 ABSTRACT.** Brainstorm shortlist (`state/resist-laser-brainstorm.md`, C5). The only genuine
> source-side escape — sidesteps the accelerator entirely (meta-law M2/E2). Unverified.

## Which wall
Flux + linewidth, at the source — an intrinsically narrow EUV line with no undulator/storage ring.

## Principle
Population-invert a highly-charged-ion plasma (Ni-like/Ne-like collisional, or 3-body recombination
in a rapidly-cooled plasma) so it LASES at 13.5 nm. Add magnetic confinement + waste-energy harvest
+ kHz re-inversion to turn single-shot X-ray-laser physics into average power. Narrow by
construction (no energy-spread linewidth wall, H_009), compact (no accelerator footprint, H_010).

## Falsifiable prediction
A re-invertible scheme sustains gain-length product gL ≥ 14 at ≥ 10 kHz with ≥ 1 µJ/pulse →
≥ 10 mW in-band average, with a clear scaling path toward the 167 W floor. If gL collapses at
high rep (thermal/recovery limits), falsified.

## How it would be tested
Collisional/recombination rate-equation gain model × rep-rate thermal budget; anchor gL against
known 46.9 nm capillary Ar laser and Ni-like demonstrations (reference-match opportunity). Not run.

## Honest status
Unverified (🜂); single-shot EUV/X-ray lasers exist, the HVM-average-power re-invertible version is
the open frontier. The 13.5 nm gain and the kHz thermal recovery are the twin risks.
